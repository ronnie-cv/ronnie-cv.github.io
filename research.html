Research & Projects

I'm a Research Assistant at the Economic Research Lab, where I contribute to policy-relevant economic research under PhD student mentorship. I've developed skills in data processing, econometric analysis, and independent research design using large-scale datasets in R and Stata. My research interests center on labor economics and social welfare programs, particularly how empirical analysis can inform effective policy design. Below, you'll find some projects that span economic analysis, data science, and interdisciplinary research. I believe good policy begins with rigorous questions, and through my work, I hope to answer them.

Projects

San Diego Energy Demand

COGS 108: Data Science in Practice

Predicted monthly household energy use in San Diego County using weather, seasonal, and geographic data. Tested multiple machine learning approaches, implementing a neural network model that identified location, time, and weather as strong predictors of energy demand. View on GitHub.

Economic Research Lab

Assisted with research on migration and urbanization patterns in developing countries, focusing on Colombia, Mexico and Cambodia. Analyzed impacts on labor markets and urban development. Contributed to data cleaning and preliminary econometric analysis, independently pursued a cross-country comparison between Mexico and Cambodia. Produced visualizations in Stata using IPUMS data.

Data Privacy Primer

Contributed to research on data privacy laws and acceptable use policies, analyzing regulations including CCPA, GDPR, and GLBA. Conducted literature review, synthesized legal frameworks, and collaborated with research fellows to produce a professional primer for a consulting firm. View primer

CozyLib

Designing a cozy digital reading space that integrates external APIs for book discovery and catalog management. Focused on creating warm, seasonal themes and intuitive user experience. Experimenting with front-end design principles and API integration.
