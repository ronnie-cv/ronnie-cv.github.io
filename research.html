Research & Projects

As a Research Assistant at the Economic Research Lab, I contribute to policy-relevant economic research under the mentorship of PhD students. My work focuses on evaluating the impact of government programs and analyzing social and labor trends using large-scale datasets and rigorous statistical methods. I have also completed practical coursework in applied econometrics, data analysis, and policy evaluation, which complements my hands-on research experience. My interests span policy, law, labor economics, and decision-making, particularly in understanding how data-driven insights can inform effective legal and policy design.

Portfolio

San Diego Energy Demand

COGS 108: Data Science in Practice

Predicted monthly household energy use in San Diego County using weather, seasonal, and geographic data. Tested machine learning methods, including Support Vector Regression (SVR). Our team implemented a neural network model, identifying location, time and weather are strong predictors of energy demand and providing insights into energy conservation. Full code and analysis available on GitHub.

Economic Research Lab

Assist with research on migration and urbanization patterns in developing countries, focusing on Colombia, Mexico and Cambodia, analyzing impacts on labor markets and urban development. Contributed to data cleaning and preliminary econometric analysis, independently pursued a cross-country comparison between Mexico and Cambodia. Produced visualizations using IPUMS data.

Data Privacy Primer

Contributed to research on data privacy laws and acceptable use policies, analzying regulations such as CCPA, GDPR, GLBA and their implications for businesses and consumers. Conducted literature review, synthesized legal frameworks, and collaborated with research fellows to produce a professional primer for a consulting firm.
